Frequently Asked Questions

PAYE (Pay As You Earn) is the system used in South Africa to collect income tax from employees. Employers deduct this tax from your salary before paying you, based on your earnings and the current tax tables provided by SARS.

UIF (Unemployment Insurance Fund) is a mandatory contribution in South Africa that provides short-term relief to workers when they become unemployed. Both employers and employees contribute 1% of the employee's salary, up to a ceiling of R17,712 per month (as of 2025).

According to SARS regulations, only 80% of your travel allowance is subject to PAYE tax. However, the full amount of the travel allowance is included in your take-home pay. UIF is calculated on the full amount (basic salary + travel allowance).

Our backward calculation uses numerical methods to precisely determine the gross salary required to achieve your desired take-home pay. The calculation accounts for all tax brackets, rebates, UIF contributions, and travel allowance tax benefits, providing results accurate to the cent.
